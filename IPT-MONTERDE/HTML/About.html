Vanessa Joyce Monterde

21 Years Old

MY SELF

Growing up, I felt insecure about myself, like my weight, my looks, my body, and even my smile. I used to tell God about it all the time, wishing I looked pretty and sexy like other people and wasn't born as a fat girl at all. As I got older, I realized I wasn't anyone's favorite—not my friends', siblings', or even my grandma's favorite. But I found comfort in knowing I was the favorite aunt of my nephew, even though he is 8 months old. I often felt worthless because of my insecurities, which made me miss out on a lot of opportunities because I was scared of being judged by other people. The bullying I faced made me anxious around people, so I learned to be by myself a lot. That's when I discovered that books were better friends than some people because they never judged me. When the pandemic hit, it hit me hard. After being bullied all my life, I tried to lose weight to feel better about myself. I lost 20 pounds by exercising and being vegan for a year. But now, even though I've gained some of the weight back, I'm okay with it because I'm happy and not stressing about my weight as much. Because now I'm looking for inner peace and fulfillment as I descend into the depths of self-love, where acceptance, contentment, joy, and sincerity are the most important.

21 Years Old

VANESSA

Life is full of ups and downs, and we frequently find ourselves dealing with our flaws and worries. But my challenges and worries have taught me something significant. I grew up with worries about my appearance, weight, and the fear of being judged. Nonetheless, throughout those difficult times, I found solace in tiny pleasures like my nephew and reading. Through my hardships, I learned that true happiness stems from within. I formerly sought approval by changing myself, but I understood that true contentment can only be found within ourselves, not on a scale. I've learned that seeking acceptance from others is not the path to happiness, but rather accepting ourselves without apology. So, I encourage you to accept your flaws since they are what define you as uniquely human. Let us remember that our shortcomings offer depth to our lives. Let us break free from self-doubt and fear and pursue inner serenity and happiness. May we all find the courage to love ourselves and inspire others to do the same. Because, in the end, the compassion and depth of our hearts are more important than our outward appearance.

MY Favorite Books

UZUMAKI

Uzumaki by Junji Ito is a horror manga that takes place in the cursed town of Kurouzu-cho, where residents become obsessed with spiral shapes. Kirie Goshima and her boyfriend, Shuichi Saito, witness bizarre and horrifying events as the spiral manifests in everything—from whirlwinds and snail-like transformations to grotesque human contortions. Shuichi's parents fall victim to the curse, his father driven to madness and his mother to paranoia. The town deteriorates into chaos as the spirals intensify, engulfing people and the environment in a terrifying vortex. Despite their efforts to escape, Kirie and Shuichi are ultimately consumed by the spiral’s power.
